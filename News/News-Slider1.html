CAN 2019: the 14 stadiums of moroccan nomination file

29 Nov 2018

share
print

Morocco has presented 14 stadiums of which 12 will finally be selected with CAF, if the candidature of Morocco is retained. 5 of these stadiums already exist and will have to be renovated and updated to the new CAF standards. 2 are launched or under construction (Oujda and Tetouan). 7 will have to be built. Of these 7, there will be 5 modular stadiums, whose capacity will be reduced by at least half after CAN 2019

In detail, the 14 stadiums are as follows:

  • 5 stadiums will be renovated or increased capacity: Marrakech, Agadir, Fez, Tangier and Rabat.
  • 4 non-modular stadiums: Casa I, Casa II, Oujda, Tetouan.
  • 5 modular stadiums, in the sense that construction techniques will reduce their capacity after the organization of the World Cup: Meknes, El Jadida, Nador, Ouarzazate, Marrakech II.

These modular stadiums will thus see their capacity increase from 46,000 to 25,000 places for El Jadida, Meknes and Marrakesh II, and from 46,000 to 20,000 places for Nador and Ouarzazate.

The 7 new stadiums are: Meknes, El Jadida, Nador, Ouarzazate, Marrakech II, Casablanca I, Casablanca II.

For these 7 new stadiums, the Moroccan bid committee presented drawings, a kind of perspective. It is obvious that the final product will be different because there will be architectural competitions.
