Fast travels to stadiums: Africa's first high-speed train

16 Nov 2018

  • ONCF claims that passenger will double between Tangier station (pictured) and Casablanca - from 3 to 6 million per year, providing a rapid return on investment.

French President Emmanuel Macron and Morocco's King Mohammed VI inaugurated Africa's first high-speed train line on Thursday.

These high-speed trains are flashing along the Atlantic coast of Morocco.

flagship new line connecting Tangier with Morocco's economic capital Casablanca in November.

The new trains are made by manufacturer Alstom and can reach speeds of 200 miles per hour. They will cut the journey time between the two cities by more than half -- to just over two hours. This is double the speed of South Africa's Gautrain, launched in 2012, which falls short of the criteria for high-speed rail.

The $2 billion project has been in development for a decade, funded by the governments of Morocco, France, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and the UAE.

The Tangiers-Casablanca route is expected to generate a sharp increase in passenger numbers that will have an enormous impact in CAN2019 in terms of traveling duration of the fans from all over mother Africa,from stadium to another, and with it support wider economic growth in the cities.
